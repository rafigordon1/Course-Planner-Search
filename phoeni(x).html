Mathematical Phoenix

Inspired by Hamid Naderi Yeganeh.

This image is composed of 10,000 circles, drawn progressively. The centers (X, Y) and radius (R) of each k-th circle are defined by a complex system of parametric equations to reflect an intricate phoenix.

X(k) = (1-W_w-W_t) ⋅ (320 + 380 sin(12π k/N)10 ⋅ sin(16π k/N) + ...) + W_t ⋅ (Tail_X(k)) + W_w ⋅ (Wing_X(k)) + Spine_X(k) Y(k) = (1-W_w-W_t) ⋅ (Y_off + (k/N) ⋅ 400 + 120 cos(8π k/N)14 ⋅ ...) + W_t ⋅ (Tail_Y(k)) + W_w ⋅ (Wing_Y(k)) + Spine_Y(k)
