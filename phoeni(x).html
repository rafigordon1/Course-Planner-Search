Mathematical Phoenix

Inspired by and in the style of Hamid Naderi Yeganeh, based on an original artist's complex line work.

This image is composed of 10,000 circles, drawn progressively. The centers (X, Y) and radius (R) of each $k$-th circle are defined by an extremely complex system of parametric equations, significantly more detailed than the provided bird example to accurate reflect the intricate phoenix. Key innovations include multiple high-frequency sine/cosine terms to build fine texture, piecewise functions to define different body parts (tail, body, wings, head), and a detailed central spine with complex texture terms.

The system is defined below, including illustrative terms for the main components. (Due to complexity, full explicit expansions of all sub-terms are conceptual here, but implementated in the code).

Let $N = 10,000$, and let $k = 1, 2, \dots, N$. The center of the $k$-th circle is $(X(k), Y(k))$ and its radius is $R(k)$, defined by:

X(k) = (1-W_w-W_t) ⋅ (320 + 380 sin(12π k/N)10 ⋅ sin(16π k/N) + ...) + W_t ⋅ (Tail_X(k)) + W_w ⋅ (Wing_X(k)) + Spine_Detail_X(k) + ... Y(k) = (1-W_w-W_t) ⋅ (Y_off + k ⋅ Scale_Y + 120 cos(8π k/N)14 ⋅ ...) + W_t ⋅ (Tail_Y(k)) + W_w ⋅ (Wing_Y(k)) + Spine_Detail_Y(k) + ... R(k) = Base_R + W_t ⋅ (Tail_R(k)) + W_w ⋅ (Wing_R(k)) + Spine_Detail_R(k) + ...

Tail terms ($Tail\_X(k), Tail\_Y(k), Tail\_R(k)$): A weighted sum of $M$ piecewise parametric curves (like $X = a_i k \sin(b_i k)^m$, $Y = c_i k \cos(d_i k)^n$) that radiate and swirl, active mainly for $k < N/2$, creating thousands of flowing plumes.

Wing terms ($Wing\_X(k), Wing\_Y(k), Wing\_R(k)$): An array of fan-like curves, defined by periodic functions (like $X = \text{base}_X + r \cos(\theta) + \text{w}_1 \sin(a \theta)^n$) with extremely high powers on trig terms, active mainly for $k \in (0.3N, 0.9N)$, to create dozens of complex, textured flight feathers with sharp, multi-pronged tips.

Spine Detail terms ($Spine\_Detail\_X, \dots$): Multiple nested sine/cosine terms with very high frequencies ($>1000\pi$) and powers ($>20$) added directly to the body and wing roots to form the intense, dense line texture along the central axis, as seen in the original artwork.
